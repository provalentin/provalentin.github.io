# Interview Preparation: Salary Negotiation & Offer Guide

**Read time:** 9 min | **Critical:** Yes

## Before Negotiation

### Research Salaries

```
Resources:
├─ Levels.fyi (tech salaries by company/level)
├─ Blind (anonymous salary data)
├─ Glassdoor
├─ PayScale
├─ LinkedIn Salary

Example (Senior Software Engineer at FAANG in SF):
├─ Base: $180K - $220K
├─ Stock: $300K - $500K
├─ Bonus: 15-20%
├─ Total: $500K - $750K
```

### Know Your Worth

```
Factors:
├─ Experience level (years, seniority)
├─ Skills (languages, expertise, specialization)
├─ Location (SF > NYC > other cities)
├─ Industry (FAANG > startups > others)
├─ Company stage (public > late stage > early)
└─ Market conditions (booming vs. downturn)

Use: salary_component_ranges × your_multiplier
```

### Get Written Offer First

```
Never negotiate verbally
Always ask for written offer:
"Thank you for the offer! Can you send it in writing
so I can review all components?"

Gives time to:
├─ Calculate total compensation
├─ Research market rate
├─ Prepare negotiation
└─ Review with trusted advisors
```

## Negotiation Strategy

### Don't Accept First Offer

```
❌ "That sounds great, I accept!"

✓ "Thank you for the offer! I'm excited about the role.
   I'd like to discuss compensation. Based on my experience
   and market research, I was expecting [X]. Can we discuss?"

This shows:
├─ Confidence
├─ Professionalism
└─ Market awareness
```

### Negotiate All Components

```
Don't just focus on base salary:

Base: $200K
Stock (4-year vesting): $400K
Sign-on bonus: $50K
Annual bonus: 20%
Relocation: $25K
Remote: Negotiate flexibility
PTO: Unlimited?
Benefits: 401K match?

Total package often > base × 2
```

### Anchoring

```
Anchor high (but realistic):
"Based on my experience and market research,
I was expecting $250K base + $500K stock."

If they counter at $220K:
You're thrilled (it's more than their opening)
vs.
If you had anchored at $230K:
You're disappointed (less than your anchor)

High anchor (if reasonable) improves outcome
```

### Let Them Counter

```
You: "I was expecting $250K base"
Them: "We can do $220K + $50K sign-on"

This is progress! Let them feel like they got you something.
It leads to better outcomes than "take it or leave it"
```

## Tactics

### The Pause

```
They make offer: "$200K base"
You: [silence, count to 3]

Awkward silence often prompts:
"Or we could do $210K..."

Don't rush to accept. Let them wonder.
```

### Specificity

```
❌ Weak: "I'd like more"
✓ Strong: "Based on market rates and my experience,
           I'd like $220K base and $450K stock"

Specific number shows you did research
Creates anchor for negotiation
```

### Multiple Offers

```
If you have multiple offers:
"I have another offer at $250K base from Company B.
I prefer Company A, but the compensation gap is significant.
Can you compete?"

Most companies will compete for good candidates
Shows your market value
```

### Don't Disclose Previous Salary

```
Illegal in many places to ask
If they ask: "I'm focused on fair market value for this role,
            not my previous salary"

If they insist: "My last role paid [X], but that's not
               relevant to this different role at different company"
```

## Equity Considerations

### Stock Options (Startup)

```
Offer: 0.5% equity over 4 years

Questions:
├─ When does vesting start? (immediate or 1-year cliff?)
├─ What's the strike price?
├─ How many shares total? (what % is this?)
├─ Latest valuation? (equity = cash × valuation)
├─ Fully diluted shares? (after all options exercised)

0.5% worth:
├─ If company valued $10M = $50K
├─ If company valued $100M = $500K
└─ If company valued $1B = $5M

Huge range! Ask about valuation and future exit.
```

### Restricted Stock Units (RSU) - Public Companies

```
Offer: $400K RSU vest over 4 years

Vesting schedule (typical):
└─ 25% year 1 (cliff)
└─ 25% year 2
└─ 25% year 3
└─ 25% year 4

Tax implications:
├─ Taxed as income when vested (not when granted)
└─ Price may change after vesting date
```

## Common Mistakes

```
❌ Accepting first offer immediately
❌ Only negotiating base salary
❌ Not researching market rates
❌ Disclosing previous salary
❌ Negotiating emotionally
❌ Rejecting offer over $5K when total difference is $50K
❌ Not getting in writing
❌ Accepting without understanding equity
```

## Red Flags

```
Company won't negotiate:
"Take it or leave it" → Walk away

Stock/options with no liquidity event:
Private company, no IPO plans → Equity worth $0

Promises without documentation:
"We'll increase bonus later" → Not guaranteed

If multiple red flags, probably not worth it
```

## Negotiation Script

```
Recruiter: "We're offering $200K base, $300K stock"

You: "Thank you! I'm excited about the role.
     I've researched market rates and my experience.
     I was expecting $230K base and $450K stock.
     Based on your constraints, what's the best you can do?"

Them: "We can do $215K and $400K"

You: "That's closer. What about $225K and $425K?"

Them: "We can stretch to $220K and $425K"

You: "I appreciate the effort. Given my experience with [X],
     the role impact on [Y], I think $225K is fair.
     Can we land on $225K and $425K?"

Them: "Okay, $225K and $425K."

You: "Thank you! I'm excited to join the team.
     Can you send me the updated offer in writing?"
```

## After Acceptance

```
Get everything in writing:
├─ Offer letter with all components
├─ Stock/equity agreement
├─ Signing bonus timing
└─ Start date

Don't burn bridges:
├─ Give proper notice (2 weeks is standard)
├─ Transition your work
├─ Offer to help knowledge transfer
└─ Keep good relationships (industry is small)
```
